# POWER OF ATTORNEY

This Power of Attorney ("POA") is executed on [Date] by:

**PRINCIPAL:** [Full Name], residing at [Address] ("Principal")

**AGENT:** [Full Name], residing at [Address] ("Agent" or "Attorney-in-Fact")

## 1. GRANT OF AUTHORITY

The Principal hereby appoints the Agent as true and lawful attorney-in-fact with full power and authority to act on behalf of the Principal.

## 2. TYPE OF POWER OF ATTORNEY

☐ **GENERAL POWER OF ATTORNEY** - Broad powers over all matters

☐ **LIMITED/SPECIAL POWER OF ATTORNEY** - Specific powers listed below:

[List specific powers granted, e.g., "To sell real property located at...", "To manage bank accounts at...", etc.]

☐ **DURABLE POWER OF ATTORNEY** - Remains effective even if Principal becomes incapacitated

☐ **SPRINGING POWER OF ATTORNEY** - Becomes effective only upon Principal's incapacity

## 3. SPECIFIC POWERS GRANTED

The Agent shall have the power to:

### A. Financial Matters
- Open, close, and manage bank accounts
- Deposit and withdraw funds
- Sign checks and negotiable instruments
- File and pay taxes
- Manage investments and securities

### B. Real Property
- Buy, sell, lease, or mortgage real estate
- Manage and maintain property
- Collect rents and pay expenses
- Execute deeds and other conveyance documents

### C. Personal and Family Matters
- Apply for government benefits
- Manage retirement accounts
- Make educational decisions
- Handle insurance matters

### D. Legal Proceedings
- Initiate or defend legal actions
- Settle claims and disputes
- Hire attorneys and professionals
- Execute legal documents

## 4. EFFECTIVE DATE

This Power of Attorney shall become effective:

☐ Immediately upon execution
☐ On [Specific Date]
☐ Upon the Principal's incapacity (certified by [Number] licensed physician(s))

## 5. TERMINATION

This Power of Attorney shall terminate upon:

- Written revocation by the Principal
- Death of the Principal
- Death or incapacity of the Agent (unless successor Agent named)
- Completion of the specific purpose (for Limited POA)
- Court order terminating this Power of Attorney

## 6. SUCCESSOR AGENT

If the primary Agent is unable or unwilling to serve, the Principal appoints:

**SUCCESSOR AGENT:** [Full Name], residing at [Address]

## 7. COMPENSATION

☐ The Agent shall serve without compensation
☐ The Agent shall be compensated at the rate of $[Amount] per [hour/year]

## 8. GOVERNING LAW

This Power of Attorney shall be governed by the laws of the State of [State].

## 9. THIRD-PARTY RELIANCE

Any third party receiving a copy of this document may rely on it and act under it. Any revocation must be delivered in writing to the third party to be effective against them.
